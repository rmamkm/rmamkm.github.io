The American Civil War

1861 to 1865

As a decisive period in American history, the Civil War undoubtedly shaped the country in many ways. Fundamental human rights, politics, commerce, military strategy, technology and medicine were greatly influenced during this pivotal time. With over 600,000 casualties, the Civil War had tremendous impact to all aspects of life in the country and owes its inception to secession of states and the formation of the Confederate States of America.

Secession was prompted for many reasons, but Abraham Lincoln’s election to the office of President was one clear factor. For the South, Lincoln’s Presidency signaled an end to slavery, which would upend its economic and societal landscape.

In 1861, shortly after Lincoln's election, the Confederate States of America was established and Jefferson Davis elected its President. With the US under the command of Lincoln and the Confederate States under Davis, the country went to war and would remain in battle for 4 years.

War raged across the country, from the Battle of Antietam in Maryland to the Battle of Bear River in Idaho. Most battles were waged in the State of Virginia, which would ultimately play host to its final decisive battle on April 9th, 1865. The Battle of Appomattox Court House ushered the surrender of The Confederate States and the shaping of our modern day country.
